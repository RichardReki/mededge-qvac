# Migraine

## Overview
Migraine is a neurological condition characterized by recurrent episodes of moderate to severe headache, often on one side of the head and frequently described as throbbing. Attacks can last from a few hours to several days and are commonly accompanied by sensitivity to light and sound. Some people experience an "aura," such as visual disturbances, before the headache begins.

## Common symptoms
- Throbbing or pulsing headache, often one-sided
- Sensitivity to light, sound, or smells
- Nausea and sometimes vomiting
- Visual aura such as flashing lights or blind spots
- Worsening pain with physical activity
- Fatigue and difficulty concentrating

## Common causes / risk factors
- Family history of migraine
- Hormonal changes, such as around menstruation
- Stress or sudden relaxation after stress
- Certain foods, caffeine, or alcohol
- Irregular sleep, skipped meals, or dehydration
- Bright lights, strong smells, or weather changes

## Typical management
- Identify and avoid personal triggers using a headache diary
- Maintain regular sleep, meals, and hydration
- Acute relief with simple pain relievers (such as NSAIDs or paracetamol) and anti-nausea medication
- Migraine-specific acute medication classes such as triptans
- Preventive medication classes for frequent attacks, including certain beta-blockers, some antidepressants, anti-seizure medicines, and CGRP-targeted therapies
- Rest in a quiet, dark room during an attack

## When to seek urgent care
- A sudden, severe "thunderclap" headache unlike any before
- Headache with fever, stiff neck, rash, or confusion
- Weakness, numbness, difficulty speaking, or vision loss
- Headache after a head injury
- New or worsening headaches after age 50
