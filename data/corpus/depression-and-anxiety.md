# Depression and Anxiety

## Overview
Depression and anxiety are common mental health conditions that often occur together. Depression involves persistent low mood and loss of interest, while anxiety involves excessive worry and fear that can be hard to control. Both can affect daily functioning, sleep, and physical health, and both are treatable with the right support.

## Common symptoms
- Persistent sadness, low mood, or loss of interest in activities
- Excessive worry, restlessness, or a sense of dread
- Difficulty sleeping or sleeping too much
- Fatigue and low energy
- Trouble concentrating or making decisions
- Changes in appetite or weight
- Physical symptoms such as a racing heart, muscle tension, or stomach upset

## Common causes / risk factors
- Family history of mental health conditions
- Stressful life events, trauma, or loss
- Chronic illness or chronic pain
- Social isolation or lack of support
- Alcohol or substance use
- Imbalances in brain chemistry and hormonal changes

## Typical management
- Talking therapies such as cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT)
- Regular physical activity, good sleep habits, and stress-reduction techniques
- Staying socially connected and limiting alcohol
- Medication classes when needed, such as SSRIs and SNRIs
- A combination of therapy and medication for moderate to severe cases
- Regular follow-up to review progress and adjust treatment

## When to seek urgent care
- Thoughts of suicide or self-harm, or making a plan to act on them
- Thoughts of harming others
- Inability to care for oneself, such as not eating or drinking
- Severe panic that feels unmanageable
- Hearing or seeing things that others do not, or losing touch with reality

If you or someone you know is in immediate danger, contact local emergency services or a crisis helpline right away.
