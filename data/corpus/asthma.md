# Asthma

## Overview
Asthma is a chronic condition in which the airways in the lungs become inflamed and narrowed, making breathing difficult. Symptoms tend to come and go and can be triggered by allergens, exercise, or respiratory infections. With proper management, most people with asthma can lead active, normal lives.

## Common symptoms
- Wheezing (a whistling sound when breathing)
- Shortness of breath
- Chest tightness
- Coughing, often worse at night or early morning
- Symptoms that worsen with exercise or exposure to triggers

## Common causes / risk factors
- Family history of asthma, eczema, or allergies
- Exposure to allergens such as pollen, dust mites, mold, or pet dander
- Tobacco smoke and air pollution
- Respiratory infections, especially in childhood
- Cold air and physical exertion
- Workplace irritants and certain chemicals

## Typical management
- Identify and avoid personal triggers where possible
- Use a personalized asthma action plan and track symptoms
- Reliever (rescue) inhalers, typically short-acting beta-agonists, for quick symptom relief
- Preventer (controller) inhalers, typically inhaled corticosteroids, taken regularly to reduce inflammation
- Combination inhalers (inhaled corticosteroid plus long-acting beta-agonist) for ongoing control
- Stop smoking and keep vaccinations up to date to reduce respiratory infections
- Regular reviews to check inhaler technique and control

## When to seek urgent care
- Severe breathlessness that makes speaking in full sentences difficult
- Reliever inhaler not helping or needed more often than usual
- Lips or fingertips turning blue
- Rapidly worsening wheeze, cough, or chest tightness
- Exhaustion, confusion, or drowsiness during an attack
