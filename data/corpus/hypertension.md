# Hypertension

## Overview
Hypertension, or high blood pressure, is a long-term condition in which the force of blood pushing against the artery walls stays elevated over time. A normal reading is generally around 120/80 mmHg, while readings persistently at or above 130/80 mmHg are usually considered raised. Because it often causes no symptoms, hypertension is sometimes called a "silent" condition that is detected only through measurement.

## Common symptoms
- Usually no noticeable symptoms (often found during routine checks)
- Occasional headaches, especially with very high readings
- Dizziness or lightheadedness
- Blurred vision in severe cases
- Nosebleeds (uncommon and non-specific)

## Common causes / risk factors
- Older age and family history of high blood pressure
- Excess body weight and physical inactivity
- High dietary sodium (salt) intake
- Heavy alcohol use and smoking
- Chronic stress and poor sleep
- Underlying kidney or hormonal conditions (secondary hypertension)

## Typical management
- Reduce dietary sodium and follow a DASH-style eating pattern rich in fruits, vegetables, and whole grains
- Maintain a healthy weight and exercise regularly (aim for regular moderate activity most days)
- Limit alcohol, stop smoking, and manage stress
- Monitor blood pressure at home and attend regular reviews
- Common medication classes when needed: ACE inhibitors, angiotensin receptor blockers (ARBs), calcium channel blockers, and thiazide-type diuretics
- Treatment is often combined with managing other risks such as cholesterol and blood sugar

## When to seek urgent care
- Blood pressure readings that are extremely high (for example, 180/120 mmHg or above)
- Severe headache with confusion or vision changes
- Chest pain, shortness of breath, or irregular heartbeat
- Sudden weakness, numbness, or difficulty speaking (possible stroke)
- Severe back or abdominal pain
